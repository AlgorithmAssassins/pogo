Basketball

Basketball has a rich history at the Olympic Games and is one of the most popular and highly anticipated events during the Summer Olympics.

Swimming

Swimming is one of the most popular and widely watched events at the Summer Olympics. It has been a part of the Olympic Games program since the first modern Olympics in 1896.

Gymnastics

Gymnastics is a captivating and highly popular sport at the Summer Olympics, showcasing the remarkable athleticism, grace, and precision of its participants.

Curling

Curling is a fascinating and unique winter sport that has been a part of the Winter Olympics since the 1998 Nagano Games.

Bobsleigh

Bobsleigh, often referred to as simply "bobsled," is an exciting and fast-paced winter sport that has been part of the Winter Olympics since the first Winter Games in 1924.

Skiing

Skiing is a popular and diverse sport featured prominently in the Winter Olympics. The term "skiing" encompasses various disciplines, each with its own unique challenges and thrills.

Snow Skateboarding

Skateboarding made its debut as an Olympic sport at the 2020 Tokyo Summer Olympics, which were held in 2021.

Ice Hockey

A high-octane team sport, ice hockey pits players against each other in fast-paced action, as they fiercely compete to score goals and defend their net with precision and passion.

Wheelchair Rugby

Also known as "murderball," wheelchair rugby is a highly physical and intense team sport for athletes with impairments affecting both the upper and lower limbs.

Sitting Volleyball

Sitting volleyball is a modified version of able-bodied volleyball, designed for athletes with physical disabilities, particularly those who cannot stand or have limited mobility.

Wheelchair Basketball

Wheelchair basketball is a fast-paced and exciting sport played by athletes with physical disabilities who use wheelchairs.

Cycling

Pedal to victory as cyclists race on various terrains, showcasing endurance and speed in individual and team events, pushing the limits of human physicality.

Para-Cycling

Para-cycling is a cycling sport adapted for athletes with physical disabilities. There are various classifications within para-cycling, depending on the type and extent of the impairment.

Football (Soccer)

The beautiful game unfolds on the field, where players showcase their soccer prowess, teamwork, and goal-scoring abilities, captivating fans worldwide.

Volleyball

Volleyball is a popular and fast-paced team sport played between two teams, each consisting of six players. The objective of the game is to score points by sending the ball over the net and into the opponent's court.

Triathlon

Triathlon is a multi-sport endurance event that combines swimming, cycling, and running.

Wrestling

Wrestling is a combat sport that involves grappling and controlling an opponent to win by pinning them to the ground or scoring points through various techniques.

Karate

Karate is a martial art that combines striking and blocking techniques. The Youth Olympic Games include karate events, offering a platform for young karatekas to demonstrate their precise and powerful moves while competing against opponents from different countries.

Table Tennis

Table tennis, also known as ping pong, is a fast-paced racket sport played on a table divided by a net. Players use small paddles to hit a lightweight ball back and forth, aiming to score points by forcing their opponent to miss or hit the ball out of bounds.

Shooting

Shooting is a precision sport that involves using firearms to hit specified targets from varying distances.

Taekwondo

Taekwondo is a Korean martial art known for its high-flying kicks and dynamic strikes.
